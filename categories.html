    • TURTLE CATEGORIES

    Sea turtles are a diverse group of turtles that inhabit the coastal and marine environments of Sri Lanka. They are known for their streamlined bodies and flipper-like limbs, which make them excellent swimmers. Sri Lanka is home to several species of sea turtles, including the Green Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and Olive Ridley Turtle.

    GREEN TURTLE

    Green Tutrle(Chelonia mydas) is one of the most commonly found turtle species in Sri Lanka. They are so named because of the presence of the green fat tissues beneath their upper shell. While the younger ones are mostly carnivorous, the adult ones are herbivorous. Green turtles can grow up to a length of 1 meter and can weigh around 250 kilograms. Adult female green turtles are known to lay off around 120 to 140 eggs at a time. The best time to locate them is night as they usually wander around the shore in the darkness.

    LOGGERHEAD TURTLE

    This is a rare turtle species found in Sri Lanka and is usually identified by the length of their heads. The Loggerhead turtles(Caretta caretta) typically appear red and brown in color and possess the ability to grow as long as 1 meter and weigh around 170 to 200 kg. These are primarily carnivorous as they make the best use of their muscular jaws for crushing both crustaceans and mollusks.

    HAWKSBILL TURTLE

    This species of the Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata) of Sri Lanka is accounted to be rarest amongst the others and is often observed to reach the height of 90 cm. Their weights vary significantly between 50 to 70 kg and are identified by their bird-like-beak protruding from their narrow head. They make the best use of their beak for catching the animals hiding deep under the small crevices. These turtles are tagged as regular Sri Lanka visitors. Also, their shells are beautiful, with around 13 symmetrical vibrant pieces.

    OLIVE RIDLEY TURTLE

    Olive ridley turtles (Lepidochelys olivacea) are the smallest and most abundant of the sea turtle species. They gather in large groups, known as arribadas, for synchronized nesting events. While nesting is less frequent in Sri Lanka, olive ridley turtles can be spotted in the country's waters.

    LEATHERBACK TURTLE

    The Leatherback Turtles(Dermochelys coriacea) are the largest of all the critically endangered turtle species across the globe. They are accounted as one of the rarest finds in and around the soils of Sri Lanka. The identification of these leatherback turtles is made from their front flippers and its intricately carved shell reflecting black and white stripes.It has got a thin, rubbery and tough carapace which imparts its characteristic leathery touch to these turtles. Besides, these turtles are known to possess a blood circulatory system, which reflects that of a cold-blooded reptile. This helps the marine animal to keep their body warm with the aid of the metabolic heat of their muscle activity. These animals can reach up to 3 meters in length and weigh around 750 kg.
