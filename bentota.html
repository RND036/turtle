    BENTHOTA HACHERY

    The Bentota Turtle Hatchery, located in Bentota, Sri Lanka, is a renowned facility dedicated to the conservation and protection of sea turtles. It serves as a sanctuary for these magnificent creatures, offering a safe environment for nesting, hatching, and nurturing baby turtles before their release into the ocean.The hatchery actively works to combat the threats faced by sea turtles, including habitat destruction, pollution, poaching, and accidental capture by fishermen. Its primary focus is on collecting sea turtle eggs that are at risk in the wild and relocating them to protected areas within the hatchery. This helps to ensure the eggs' survival and increases the chances of successfully releasing the hatchlings into the ocean.The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is home to several species of sea turtles, including the Green Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. Each species has its own designated tanks or enclosures that mimic their natural habitats, providing a nurturing environment for their growth and development.Visitors to the hatchery have the opportunity to witness the various stages of the sea turtles' life cycle. They can observe the collected eggs in specially designated hatcheries, where they are carefully monitored and protected until they hatch. Hatchlings are then kept in separate tanks, allowing them to gain strength and grow under the hatchery's care.Educational programs are an integral part of the Bentota Turtle Hatchery's mission. Knowledgeable staff members provide informative presentations, explaining the life cycle of sea turtles, their behaviors, and the importance of their conservation. Visitors, particularly children, are encouraged to actively engage in learning activities to foster a deeper understanding of the issues surrounding sea turtle conservation.One of the highlights of visiting the Bentota Turtle Hatchery is the opportunity to participate in the release of baby turtles into the ocean. This hands-on experience allows visitors to play a direct role in the conservation efforts by giving the young turtles a fighting chance at survival in their natural habitat.The Bentota Turtle Hatchery also collaborates with local communities, fishermen, and government organizations to raise awareness about sustainable fishing practices and the importance of protecting sea turtles and their habitats. By working together, they strive to ensure the long-term survival of these incredible creatures.In summary, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery is an important conservation facility that promotes the preservation of sea turtles in Sri Lanka. Through its efforts, it provides a safe haven for these endangered creatures, educates the public, and actively contributes to the conservation of these remarkable species.

    KANDE VIHARAYA

    Kande Viharaya is a major Buddhist temple in Kalutara District, Sri Lanka. The temple has got its name 'Kande Viharaya' (Mountain temple) as it is built on top of a hill located near to Aluthgama town. The temple has been formally recognised by the Government as an archaeological site in Sri Lanka.The temple was founded by ven. Karapagala Dewamitta thero in 1734,under the guidance of ven. Udugama Chandrasara thero who was then chief incumbent of Galapatha Raja Maha Vihara and a pupil of ven. Weliwita Sri Saranankara Thero. Since then the temple was renovated and developed time to time under state patronage that lead the temple to become a major Buddhist shrine in the country. In 1904 the Kshethrarama Pirivena, an educational institution for monks, was attached to the temple as a result of the efforts done by Ven. Hikkaduwe Sri Sumangala Thero.The pirivena acts as a center for Buddhist education in the country, is also conducted by the Kande Vihara.The image house, Stupa and the sacred Bodhi tree with its enclosing octagonal shaped wall are considered as very old monuments in the temple. The Bodhi tree is believed to be more than 300 years old.The large Stupa of the temple which was erected in 1935, has been constructed as enclosing a small sized Stupa, constructed by Ven. Udugama Chandrasara Thero in 1783.In 2002, a foundation stone for a gigantic Samadhi Buddha statue depicting the Bhoomisparsha Mudra was laid in the temple premises and the completed statue was open to the public in 2007, making the temple more popular among the local devotees. The statue is 48.8 m (160 ft) in height and considered as one of tallest sitting Buddha statues in the world

    BENTHOTA BEACH

    Bentota Beach is a beautiful coastal destination located in the southwestern part of Sri Lanka. It is one of the most popular beach resorts in the country, known for its pristine sandy shores, clear turquoise waters, and a range of recreational activities.Stretching along the Indian Ocean, Bentota Beach features a long, wide shoreline that attracts both locals and tourists seeking sun, sand, and sea. The beach is characterized by its soft golden sands, which are perfect for leisurely walks, sunbathing, or simply relaxing under the warm tropical sun.The crystal-clear waters of Bentota Beach are inviting and ideal for swimming, making it a haven for water enthusiasts. The calm and gentle waves offer a safe environment for visitors of all ages to enjoy a refreshing dip. If you prefer more thrilling water activities, Bentota also offers options for jet skiing, water skiing, banana boat rides, and windsurfing.For those seeking a bit of adventure, Bentota Beach is a great place for snorkeling and diving. The nearby coral reefs and diverse marine life make it an excellent spot to explore the underwater world and discover colorful fish and other fascinating creatures.

    BRIEF GARDEN

    Brief Garden, also known as Lunuganga, is a renowned garden located in Bentota, Sri Lanka. It was the former country estate of the famous Sri Lankan architect Geoffrey Bawa.Covering an area of around 25 acres, Brief Garden is a stunning creation that combines natural beauty with artistic elements. The garden is a masterpiece designed by Geoffrey Bawa himself, who transformed the estate into a living work of art over several decades.The garden features a harmonious blend of tropical vegetation, vibrant flowers, and meandering pathways that lead visitors through its enchanting landscape. Lush foliage, tall trees, and colorful blooms create a serene and visually captivating environment.As you explore Brief Garden, you'll encounter various architectural structures, sculptures, and artistic installations that are seamlessly integrated into the natural surroundings. These elements represent Bawa's unique design philosophy, which merged traditional Sri Lankan elements with modern influences.The garden is divided into different sections, each with its own distinct charm. From manicured lawns and ponds adorned with water lilies to hidden nooks and terraces offering panoramic views, Brief Garden offers a range of tranquil spaces to relax and immerse yourself in nature's beauty.
